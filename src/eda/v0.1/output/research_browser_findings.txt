1. M-SCORE THRESHOLD ANALYSIS
================================================================================

FINDING: -2.22 is validated for Indian markets. No India-adjusted threshold exists.

Key Evidence:
- Jain & Bose (2023, Academy of Marketing Studies Journal): 10 years of NSE 500
  data, confirmed M-Score predictive validity in India. Used -1.78 (8-var model).
- GMT Research (Asia-wide, 3,600 companies): 33% scored above -2.22, only 16%
  above -1.78. Suggests -2.22 may be slightly lenient for Asian markets.
- Shah, Saraswat & Mehta (2018, Indian Journal of Finance): BSE 100, 65 usable
  companies. 11 (~17%) classified as likely manipulators.
- Marcellus Investment Managers: Forensic screens reject ~40% of BSE 500. Adds
  ~5.5% p.a. to back-tested returns.
- No researcher has proposed an India-calibrated alternative threshold.

Three-Zone Interpretation:
  M > -1.78  → Likely manipulator (8-variable model)
  -2.22 to -1.78 → Possible manipulator
  M < -2.22  → Unlikely manipulator (5-variable model)

Most Significant Variables for India (Shah et al.):
  1. TATA (Total Accruals to Total Assets) — strongest predictor
  2. DSRI (Days' Sales in Receivables Index)
  3. SGI (Sales Growth Index)
  Note: TATA, DSRI, SGI do NOT require COGS/SGA data.

DECISION: Keep -2.22 threshold. It is the standard for the 5-variable model and
validated across multiple Indian studies.

2. CCR (CASH CONVERSION RATIO = CFO / EBITDA) BENCHMARKS
================================================================================

FINDING: 0.80 is the practitioner consensus threshold for Indian equities.

Key Evidence:
- Marcellus Investment Managers: Average BSE 500 company converts 100% of
  operating profits to cash over 6-year lookback (CCR = 1.0x median).
  Companies in bottom 3 deciles delivered -13% CAGR.
- MoneyMuscle: "Total CFO should be at least 70-80% of Total EBITDA or PAT
  over 3-5 years."
- Screener.in community: CFO/EBITDA > 0.80 is the most common quality screen.
- Dr. Vijay Malik: Cumulative 10-year CFO must exceed cumulative 10-year PAT.
- Manpasand Beverages: 69% conversion flagged by Marcellus → later proved
  fraudulent.

Estimated Pass Rates (non-financial NSE 500 companies):
  CCR >= 0.50  →  ~70-75% pass
  CCR >= 0.60  →  ~60-65% pass
  CCR >= 0.80  →  ~45-55% pass  ← RECOMMENDED THRESHOLD
  CCR >= 1.00  →  ~35-40% pass

Sector-Wise Indicative Ranges:
  IT Services:     0.80 - 1.10+ (highest quality)
  FMCG:           0.75 - 0.95
  Pharma:         0.55 - 0.80
  Auto:           0.45 - 0.70
  Metals/Mining:  0.35 - 0.60 (lowest, capex-heavy)
  Banking/NBFC:   N/A (not applicable)

Red Flag Zones:
  CCR < 0.60-0.70  → Warning
  CCR < 0.50       → Danger zone / possible accounting manipulation

DECISION: Restore CCR threshold to 0.80 (from current 0.50). The data quality
fixes (sentinel -1.0 for unavailable data + banking exemption) now handle the
root cause. 0.50 was an overcorrection — research clearly supports 0.80.

Best Practice: Use cumulative multi-year CCR (6-year per Marcellus, 10-year
per Dr. Malik) rather than single-year. [FUTURE ENHANCEMENT]

3. BANKING / FINANCIAL SECTOR HANDLING
================================================================================

FINDING: Universal consensus — banks/NBFCs/insurance must be excluded from
M-Score AND CCR screening.

Reasons:
- Cash is raw material for banks. Lending = cash outflow in operating activities.
  Growing loan book = structurally negative OCF (healthy, not problematic).
- EBITDA is meaningless — interest income is revenue, interest expense is COGS.
- Beneish's original paper excluded financial institutions.
- Every Indian study excludes banking/financial services.
- Marcellus uses 11 separate ratios for financial companies.

Alternative Metrics for Banks/NBFCs:
  - GNPA Ratio < 3-4%
  - NNPA Ratio < 1-2%
  - Provisioning Coverage Ratio (PCR) > 70%
  - Capital Adequacy Ratio (CAR) > 12%
  - Net Interest Margin (NIM) > 3%
  - CASA Ratio > 40%
  - ROA > 1.3%

DECISION: Current CCR_EXEMPT_SECTORS = {'Banking', 'Finance', 'Insurance', 'NBFC'}
is correct. Banking-specific quality metrics are a future enhancement.

4. MISSING DATA & YFINANCE ISSUES
================================================================================

FINDING: yfinance has well-documented data quality issues for Indian stocks.

Key Issues (from GitHub Issues):
- #1649: operatingCashflow, freeCashflow, ebitda all disappeared temporarily
         (Aug 2023). Yahoo data model changes without warning.
- #1265: quarterly_cashflow returned no output for certain tickers. Two
         inconsistent Yahoo data tables.
- #2584: Financial statement line items misaligned by 1 year for ALL tickers.
         Demonstrated with RELIANCE.NS. Closed as "not planned."
- #825:  INFY.NS stopped working entirely; upgrade fixed it.
- #1087: Yahoo spam detection causes None returns when scraping many tickers.
- #191, #343, #419, #465, #475: balance_sheet, cashflow return empty DataFrames.

Most Commonly Missing Fields:
  CASH FLOW:
  - operatingCashflow → frequently None/NaN
  - freeCashflow → frequently None/NaN

  INCOME STATEMENT:
  - earningsGrowth, revenueGrowth → intermittently missing

  BALANCE SHEET:
  - Generally works for large-caps when it works at all
  - Failures are typically all-or-nothing (entire DataFrame empty)

Recommended Practices:
  1. Use ticker.cashflow/balance_sheet/income_stmt DataFrames (timeSeries endpoint)
     NOT ticker.info keys (quoteSummary endpoint)
  2. Implement robust None/NaN handling with graceful degradation
  3. Cross-validate sample against Screener.in before trusting bulk data
  4. Consider jugaad-data or Screener.in scraping as secondary sources
  5. Always use latest yfinance version

DECISION: Current neutral-default approach (DSRI=1.0, AQI=1.0 when t-1 data
missing; CCR=-1.0 sentinel when CFO unavailable) is the correct strategy. This
matches the "graceful degradation" approach recommended by practitioners.
