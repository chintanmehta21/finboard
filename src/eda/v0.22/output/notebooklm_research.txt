## Q3: Hard vs Soft Gates — Which Forensic Signals Stay Hard?

QUESTION: Which of M-Score, CCR, Pledge, D/E should remain hard gates vs become soft scoring components?

ANSWER (CRITICAL FOR PIPELINE DESIGN):

1. **M-Score (Manipulation) → HARD GATE**
   - Buffett: "When management takes the low road in accounting, there is seldom just one cockroach in the kitchen"
   - False negative = permanent capital loss (Buffett's #1 rule: never risk it)
   - False positive = error of omission (perfectly acceptable to Buffett)

2. **Promoter Pledge (Governance) → HARD GATE**
   - Buffett: "We've never succeeded in making a good deal with a bad person"
   - Integrity is non-negotiable

3. **Debt/Equity (Leverage) → SOFTEN**
   - Damodaran: borrowing lowers cost of capital up to optimal point before becoming destructive
   - Buffett: accepts massive debt for capital-intensive businesses if earnings cover interest
   - Hard D/E gate creates too many false positives

4. **CCR (Cash Conversion) → SOFTEN OR REPLACE**
   - Buffett considers EBITDA "deeply flawed" — ignores real cash cost of depreciation
   - Requiring CFO to match EBITDA unfairly penalizes capital-intensive businesses
   - Suggestion: Use EBIT-based metric instead of EBITDA

ASYMMETRY: False negatives (buying bad stocks) = catastrophic drawdowns. False positives (missing good stocks) = cost nothing. "You don't need to own every good business."

## Q5: Confidence Scoring with Partial Data

QUESTION: Should price-only stocks get 0.85x confidence penalty vs 1.0x for full-data stocks?

ANSWER (IMPORTANT NUANCE):
- Buffett: Would assign 0.0x (total exclusion) — "buying based on price behavior is speculation"
- Damodaran: Also anchors to fundamentals; views them as essential, not marginal
- Both would argue fundamental data is NOT optional
- BUT: "For a quantitative momentum system, applying a confidence penalty is standard mathematical practice to manage risk and reflect higher uncertainty"
- CONCLUSION: Buffett/Damodaran philosophy conflicts with quant momentum approach. For our system, a confidence penalty (not total exclusion) is the pragmatic choice supported by quant finance practice.

## SYNTHESIS — Key Design Decisions from NotebookLM Research

1. **M-Score and Pledge: KEEP as hard gates** (but only apply to stocks WITH data)
2. **D/E and CCR: SOFTEN to scoring components** (reduce false positives)
3. **Missing fundamentals: NOT a hard exclusion** — use price signals + confidence penalty
4. **Confidence penalty: ~0.85x for price-only** (quant standard, despite Buffett/Damodaran objection)
5. **Price-only factors: Add 7-day MA, 21-day MA, MACD, Bollinger Bands** as technical indicators
6. **200 DMA, 52-week high: Need browser research** (not in notebook)
7. **CCR metric: Consider replacing EBITDA denominator with EBIT** (Buffett's EBITDA critique)
8. **False negative vs false positive asymmetry: Accept more false positives** (exclude more) on integrity signals, but allow more stocks through on financial metrics
